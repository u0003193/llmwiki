# Objections and Responses

## Objection 1 — Worship is not doctrine
### Strength
This objection rightly warns against over-reading liturgical language as direct dogmatic proof.

### Response
In Basil, worship is not isolated from Scripture. It functions as confessional manifestation within a scriptural and ecclesial argument.

## Objection 2 — Basil’s argument depends too much on later doctrinal categories
### Strength
This objection warns against anachronism.

### Response
The argument should be grounded in Basil’s own controversy, vocabulary, and textual logic, not imposed later categories.

## Objection 3 — The opponent is underdeveloped
### Strength
A weak reconstruction of the opponent would make the argument unfair.

### Response
The project must reconstruct the opponent’s strongest case before evaluating Basil’s response.

## Objection 4 — The link to Maximus may be forced
### Strength
Basil and Maximus address different contexts and questions.

### Response
The comparison should not claim identity but trace a disciplined theological trajectory from pneumatological confession to Christological anthropology.
