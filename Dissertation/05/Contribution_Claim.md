# Contribution Claim

## Current Working Claim
This dissertation argues that selected patristic sources show doctrine, worship, and human transformation to be mutually interpretive, such that Basil’s pneumatological defense of worship and Maximus’s Christological anthropology together provide a richer account of divine agency and human becoming.

## Short Version
Patristic theology shows that worship is not secondary to doctrine and that divine agency is central to human transformation.
