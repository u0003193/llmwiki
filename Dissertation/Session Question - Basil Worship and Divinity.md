# Session Question — Basil: Worship and the Divinity of the Spirit

## Main Question
How does Basil of Caesarea use worship, especially doxology, to argue for the divinity of the Holy Spirit?

## Why This Question Matters
This question is useful because *On the Holy Spirit* was written in a context where disputes over how the Spirit could be named or glorified were tied directly to disputes about the Spirit's status. Basil's opponents objected to doxological formulas and tried to use close analysis of prepositions and liturgical wording to imply hierarchy within the Trinity. Basil responds by coordinating Scripture, ecclesial usage, and theological reasoning.

## Session Boundaries
This session focuses only on the relationship between:
- worship / doxology
- the Spirit’s divine status
- baptism / salvation
- Basil’s anti-subordinationist response

It does not try to cover every possible topic in On the Holy Spirit.
