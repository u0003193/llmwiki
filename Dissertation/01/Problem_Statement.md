# Problem Statement

## Problem
Modern theological reflection often separates doctrine, worship, and human transformation into different domains. Patristic theologians such as Basil and Maximus may offer a more integrated account in which worship, divine agency, and anthropology mutually interpret one another.

## Specific Basil Problem
In Basil’s On the Holy Spirit, a dispute over doxology is not merely a dispute over wording. It reveals a deeper question: whether the Spirit’s role in worship and salvation requires full divine confession.

## Why This Problem Matters
If worship is doctrinally significant, then theology cannot treat liturgical confession as secondary. If the Spirit’s role in salvation requires full divine agency, then pneumatology directly affects anthropology and soteriology.

## Tension
Is worship a source of theological knowledge, or only an expression of doctrine already known elsewhere?
