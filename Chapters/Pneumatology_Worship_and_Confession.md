# Chapter: Pneumatology, Worship, and Confession in Basil

## Dissertation Sub-Question
How does Basil of Caesarea use worship to defend the Spirit’s divine status?

## Chapter QUD
Does worship function as doctrinal evidence in Basil’s pneumatology?

## Competing Positions
- worship is symbolic and non-doctrinal
- scriptural proof alone is sufficient
- doxology does not imply ontological status
- language distinctions preserve hierarchy rather than unity

## Chapter Thesis
In On the Holy Spirit, Basil shows that ecclesial worship—especially doxology and baptismal confession—functions as a confessional extension of scriptural truth, making reduction of the Spirit incompatible with both Christian worship and Christian salvation.
