# Does worship imply divinity?

## Anchor Text
Basil’s dispute over whether the Spirit may be glorified together with the Father and the Son.

## Why This QUD Is Justified
The controversy over doxology raises the question whether worship language merely expresses reverence or whether it bears doctrinal meaning.

## Alternative QUDs
- Does doxology prove ontology?
- Is liturgical language doctrinally binding?
- Does the Spirit’s role in baptism imply divine dignity?

## Answer Given in Text
Basil appears to argue that worship does not stand apart from doctrine; worship manifests and safeguards the Church’s confession of the Spirit.

## Grounds
- disputed doxological formulas
- baptismal naming
- Spirit’s role in sanctification and salvation
- Basil’s critique of preposition-based hierarchy

## Implication
If worship is doctrinally significant, the Spirit cannot be treated as a lower being without corrupting Christian confession.
