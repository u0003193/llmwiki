## GOAL
Analyze one biblical book from repeated reading through structure, QUD chains, and whole-book thesis.

## CONTEXT
This workflow is for doctoral-level exegesis.
Do not jump to theology or purpose statements until structural and discourse work is visible.

Use:
- the biblical text
- existing notes in Books/, Passages/, Concepts/, Questions/
- COPILOT-INSTRUCTIONS-v2.md

## EXPECTATIONS
Return:
1. thought units
2. structural clusters
3. QUD chains
4. whole-book thesis
5. purpose statement
6. notes to create or update

## PROCEDURE

### Step 1 — Whole-Book Reading Lens
Identify:
- repeated themes
- major transitions
- explicit purpose statements
- dominant tensions
- major lexical repetitions
- rhetorical turns

### Step 2 — Define Thought Units
Divide the book into coherent thought units.
For each unit explain why it belongs together.

### Step 3 — Group into Structural Clusters
Combine thought units into larger argumentative or narrative clusters.
Explain the unifying logic.

### Step 4 — Observe Discourse Logic
For each major unit identify:
- conjunctions
- contrasts
- grounds
- purpose clauses
- causation
- repetition
- progression

### Step 5 — Build QUD Chains
For each major unit or cluster identify:
- explicit or implicit question
- answer given
- grounds
- implication
- confidence level

### Step 6 — Write Section Theses
One sentence thesis per major cluster.

### Step 7 — Whole-Book Thesis
Give one sentence that captures the unified argument or exhortation.

### Step 8 — Purpose Statement
Use:
To X by Y1, by Y2, by Y3 ...

---

## RULES

- Do not skip intermediate steps
- Distinguish structure from theology
- Distinguish local unit thesis from whole-book thesis
- If a QUD is speculative, label it
- If textual or translation issues materially affect the argument, note them
