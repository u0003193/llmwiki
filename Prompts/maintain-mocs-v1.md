Purpose

Keep the MOC layer synchronized with the vault.

MOCs are not simple indexes.
They are research navigation hubs.

Their role is to connect:

- Questions
- Sources
- Concepts
- Passages
- Arguments
- Syntheses
- Chapters
- Dissertation goals

MOCs should evolve as the research evolves.

---

## GOAL

Audit and update existing MOCs so they accurately represent the current state of the vault.

## PROCEDURE

### Step 1 — Inventory Existing MOCs

List all files under:

- MOCs/

Classify:

- research-cluster MOC
- concept MOC
- dissertation MOC
- chapter MOC
- person MOC

---

### Step 2 — Detect Orphans

Find notes that:

- are not linked from any MOC
- belong to active research areas
- have status:
  - linked
  - tested
  - vetted
  - chapter-ready

For each orphan:

Provide:

File:
Reason:
Recommended MOC:

---

### Step 3 — Update Existing MOCs

For each MOC:

Check whether its sections are current:

#### Sources
#### Questions
#### Concepts
#### Passages
#### Arguments
#### Syntheses
#### Chapters
#### Open Tensions
#### Next Actions

Add missing notes.

Remove obsolete notes.

---

### Step 4 — Strengthen Navigation

Ensure:

Every MOC contains:

### Related MOCs
### Key Questions
### Core Arguments
### Current Chapter Links

If missing, recommend additions.

---

### Step 5 — Dissertation Alignment

Verify:

All research-cluster MOCs connect upward to:

[[Dissertation MOC]]

All chapter notes connect upward to:

[[Dissertation MOC]]

All synthesis notes connect to at least:

- one MOC
- one Chapter
or
- one Dissertation Question

---

### Step 6 — Recommend New MOCs

Create a new MOC only if:

- ≥5 related notes exist
OR
- the cluster supports a dissertation chapter
OR
- the cluster supports a major concept area

Examples:

- Pneumatology MOC
- Basil MOC
- Maximus MOC
- Anthropology MOC
- Worship MOC

Do not create unnecessary MOCs.

---

## LINKING RULES

Every major note should belong to at least one MOC.

Examples:

Patristic Work
→ Basil MOC

Argument
→ Argument MOC

Passage
→ Pneumatology MOC

Synthesis
→ Dissertation MOC

Chapter
→ Dissertation MOC
